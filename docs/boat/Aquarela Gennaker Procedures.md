## Summary

Standard crew commands for gennaker manoeuvres on Aquarela. These are the Italian-language commands used during racing and practice. The procedure assumes an asymmetric gennaker on a retractable bowsprit, which is the standard Nitro 80 configuration.

## Hoist (Alzata Gennaker)

Execute in this order. Helm bears away to a broad reach or run before starting.

| Step | Command (IT) | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **FUORI TACK** | Deploy the tack line — bowsprit extends forward |
| 2 | **POGGIO E VADO DI POPPA** | Helm bears away, go dead downwind or by the lee |
| 3 | **BOMPRESSO FUORI** | Confirm bowsprit is fully extended and locked |
| 4 | **DRIZZA SU** | Halyard up — hoist the gennaker to the top |
| 5 | **SCOTTA DEL GEN IN MANO** | Gennaker sheet in hand — trim crew takes the sheet |
| 6 | **FIOCCO GIÙ** | Drop the jib (furl or lower) to clear airflow |
| 7 | **VANG IN MANO** | Vang in hand — crew takes control of boom vang for mainsail trim |

## Douse (Ammainata Gennaker)

Execute in this order. Typically done before rounding a leeward mark or heading upwind.

| Step | Command (IT) | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **FIOCCO SU** | Hoist the jib (unfurl or raise) before dousing the gennaker |
| 2 | **SCOTTA FIOCCO DA BOLINA** | Trim the jib sheet to close-hauled — ready for upwind |
| 3 | **BASE GEN IN MANO** | Grab the gennaker foot/tack line — crew prepares to gather sail |
| 4 | **BOMPRESSO DENTRO** | Retract the bowsprit |
| 5 | **APRI TACK** | Release the tack — gennaker tack comes free |
| 6 | **BASE GENNAKER DENTRO** | Pull the gennaker foot inboard — gather the sail from the base up |
| 7 | **APRI DRIZZA** | Release the halyard — gennaker comes down |
| 8 | **GENNAKER NEL SACCO** | Stuff the gennaker into the bag — pack it for next hoist |

## Notes

- The Nitro 80 uses a **removable bowsprit** (bompresso) for the asymmetric gennaker. Ensure the bowsprit is fully deployed and locked before hoisting, and fully retracted before rounding upwind marks.
- The jib comes DOWN during gennaker sailing and goes UP before the douse — this is the standard sequence to maintain drive through the transition.
- The vang is critical during downwind sailing to control mainsail twist and prevent accidental gybes.
- With 5–6 crew on a Nitro 80, typical roles: helm, gennaker sheet, halyard/bowsprit, jib/vang, and tactician.
