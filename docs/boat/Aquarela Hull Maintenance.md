## Summary

Aquarela is permanently moored on Lake Lugano (freshwater). The hull already has **Thin Film Swiss** (by Bucher & Walt) applied by the previous owner, who also left an almost-full 2L can. Annual maintenance is a single re-coat over the existing thin-film — no primer, no stripping. The primary fouling threat is **zebra mussels** (Dreissena polymorpha), which require copper-based thin-film protection on stationary boats.

## Current Hull Coating Stack

From water-side inward:

1. **Thin Film Swiss antifouling** — copper dioxide + PTFE thin-film (~30 µm per coat)
2. **Primer/barrier coat** — epoxy barrier (applied at some point by previous owner)
3. **Gelcoat** — factory white polyester gelcoat (~0.5-0.8 mm)
4. **Hull laminate** — vacuum-infused fiberglass sandwich (see [[Aquarela Hull Structure]])

## Antifouling Product Details

### Thin Film Swiss (current coating)
- **Manufacturer**: Bucher & Walt, schiffsfarben.ch
- **Active ingredient**: Copper dioxide (~30% metallic copper)
- **Technology**: PTFE-based thin-film, same family as VC17m (discontinued spring 2024) and SEATEC GTI 30
- **Compatibility**: Explicitly compatible with VC17m and other PTFE thin-films
- **Racing advantage**: ~30 µm per coat vs ~250 µm for conventional paint = dramatically less drag

### SEATEC GTI 30 (compatible alternative)
- **Source**: SVB Marine (svb24.com), ~€100/2L can
- **Spec**: VC17m successor, PTFE + metallic copper
- **Use case**: If Thin Film Swiss supply runs out or for fresh stock
- **NOT needed this season** — existing can from previous owner is sufficient

## Annual Re-Coat Procedure

### When
End of winter / early spring, before launch. Or mid-season haul if fouling builds up.

### Prerequisites
- Boat hauled out (crane lift via structural lifting ring)
- D-shackle for lifting: 3¼ ton WLL minimum (3:1 safety margin over 1,100 kg displacement)

### Steps

**1. Pressure wash**
Immediately after haul-out. Remove all growth, slime, and zebra mussel colonies. Pay attention to waterline, keel-hull junction, rudder fittings, and bulb recesses.

**2. Inspect hull**
- Check gelcoat for cracks, chips, osmosis blisters (bubbles with acetic-smelling liquid)
- Check keel bolts for tightness — **structural critical point**
- Inspect keel retraction mechanism, pivot points, cables
- Check rudder bearings and pintles for play
- Inspect lead bulb for corrosion

**3. Repair gelcoat** (if needed)
- YachtCare Gelcoat Spatula VT (white RAL 9010) for chip/scratch repair
- Sand flush with 320 → 600 grit after curing

**4. Light sand** (if needed)
- Only rough or flaking spots, 320 grit
- Thin-film does NOT require full sanding between annual coats
- Wipe with acetone to remove dust and grease

**5. Apply one coat Thin Film Swiss**
- Use **short-nap foam roller** only (never brush — leaves texture that adds drag)
- Thin, even coat — the paint is meant to be ultra-thin
- Dries in minutes at room temperature
- Do NOT sand between coats if applying two

**6. Wet-sand for racing finish** (optional but recommended)
- Wait for full cure (24h minimum)
- Progressive wet-sanding: 1000 → 1500 → 2000 grit
- Creates mirror-smooth surface for maximum hull speed
- Up to ~10% speed improvement vs unfinished antifouling

**7. Polish and wax topsides**
- 3M Perfect-It system: Boat Wash → Rubbing Compound → Machine Polish → Wax
- Protects gelcoat above waterline from UV

**8. Launch**
- Thin-film can be launched same day after application
- Lower keel fully before sailing, verify lock/pin engagement

## Swiss Biocide Regulations

As of January 1, 2025, the **ChemBiozidDV** regulation applies:

- **Copper-based antifouling** requires mandatory consultation with trained personnel before purchase
- Self-service purchase prohibited for products containing dicopper oxide, copper thiocyanate, copper pyrithione
- Online purchases require telephone or video consultation
- **Workaround**: Order from SVB Marine (Germany) — ships to Switzerland without Swiss consultation requirement
- **Local option**: Bucher & Walt (schiffsfarben.ch) sells Thin Film Swiss directly and can provide required consultation

## Why Thin-Film (Not Biocide-Free) for Lake Lugano

Biocide-free silicone coatings (Hempel Silic One, YachtCare Protector Hard) work via fouling-release — organisms can't stick. However:

- **Zebra mussels** attach with byssal threads strong enough to defeat fouling-release surfaces on stationary boats
- Biocide-free only works if the boat moves regularly (self-cleaning effect)
- Aquarela is **permanently moored** → needs copper-based protection
- Thin-film is the best of both worlds: copper kills zebra mussels + PTFE gives racing-smooth surface

## Compatibility Warning

Thin-film coatings (Thin Film Swiss, GTI 30, VC17m) are **only compatible with other thin-films**. You cannot:
- Apply conventional thick antifouling over thin-film
- Apply thin-film over conventional antifouling without stripping to bare gelcoat + epoxy primer
- Switch between thin-film and silicone fouling-release without full strip

If ever switching away from thin-film: strip everything to gelcoat, apply 2-3 coats epoxy primer (SEATEC Epotec or VC Tar2), then apply new system.
