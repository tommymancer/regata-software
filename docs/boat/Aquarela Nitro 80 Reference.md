## Summary

The Nitro 80 is a French-designed 8-metre one-design sportboat/day-sailer, drawn by Jacques Fauroux and built by Chantier Caparros in Provence. It combines racing dinghy performance (planing capable, trapeze crew) with coastal cruiser practicality (retractable keel, trailerable, 2 berths). Approximately 20–24 units were built, with the fleet concentrated around Cannes and the Côte d'Azur. Aquarela is a Nitro 80 permanently moored on Lake Lugano.

## Sail Plan

| Sail | Area |
|------|------|
| Mainsail | ~20 m² |
| Jib (110%) | ~15 m² |
| Symmetric spinnaker | ~55 m² |
| Asymmetric gennaker | ~60–62 m² |

The rig is a fractional sloop without backstays. The relatively tall mast and fine waterlines give strong light-air performance. Both symmetric spinnaker (on pole) and asymmetric gennaker (on removable bowsprit) are class-legal.

## Versions

Two versions were produced on the identical hull and sail plan:

**Regata** — Very low coachroof, flush deck, open cockpit optimised for fast crew work. Minimal interior.

**Crociera** — Slightly raised coachroof for more internal volume, small companionway hatch, optional basic interior fittings. Still a spartano day-boat.

Both versions race together in the Nitro 80 class. The association conducted weight and sail measurements (2010) to ensure parity.

## History

The Nitro 80 was designed by Jacques Fauroux (prolific French naval architect) for Chantier Caparros at Puget-sur-Argens. First hulls launched in the early 2000s. Production was interrupted when a fire destroyed the original moulds. New moulds were built in 2011 at a new Caparros facility in Fréjus, with 2 new hulls completed that summer and 2 more following in early 2012. Total production: approximately 20–24 units.

The fleet's home port is primarily Cannes (Yacht Club de Cannes, CN Antibes), with isolated boats elsewhere — notably "Bonaire" at La Rochelle (the only Atlantic-based Nitro 80) and examples on Lake Geneva.

## Class Organisation

Owners are organised in the **As.Pro.Nitro** (Association des Propriétaires de Nitro 80), which manages:

- One-design class rules and measurement controls
- Annual **Challenge Nitro 80** (trophy held at YC Cannes)
- **National Nitro 80** championship (typically May)
- Handicap National (HN) "Classe L" participation

## Racing Record

The Nitro 80 has been competitive well beyond its small fleet size:

- Multiple victories in the **Championnat de France Croiseurs Légers**
- **ORC Sportsboat Europeans 2016** (Chioggia) — TNT (Frédéric Peroche) among the top finishers, competitive against Melges 24, J/70, Farr 280
- **Bol d'Or Mirabaud 2022** (Lake Geneva) — class TF3 winner (Fontan/Peroche/Ravier, YC Cannes)
- Consistently dominates local IRC/OSIRIS regattas in the Cannes area
- Participates in Ski Yachting de Cannes and various club events

Typical race crew: 5–6 persons on trapeze.

## Sailing Characteristics

The Nitro 80 is described as reactive, fast, and fun to helm. Key traits:

- Excellent light-air speed thanks to high sail area/displacement ratio
- Planes readily in fresh conditions on reaches and downwind legs
- Well-balanced helm — docile in moderate winds, demanding in heavy air
- Good upwind pointing ability
- Stiff for its size (favourable ballast ratio with 430 kg keel on ~1100 kg boat)
- Requires experienced crew in strong wind (trapeze hiking, precise trim)
- Light displacement means easy trailering and quick acceleration

## Maintenance & Refit

The Nitro 80 is robust and low-maintenance for a performance boat:

- Vacuum-infused sandwich construction is resistant to osmosis
- Retractable keel mechanism needs periodic inspection (pivot, cables, lock pin)
- Common upgrades: laminate/mylar sails replacing original dacron, modern bozzelli, updated electronics
- The class association monitors modifications to preserve one-design parity
- Hull maintenance for Aquarela specifically: see [[Aquarela Hull Maintenance]]

## Trailering

The boat was designed for road transport:

- Keel retracts to 1.40 m draught
- Structural lifting ring for crane operations
- Weight ~1,100 kg — within range for a suitable car + trailer
- Many owners use portable argano/rulliere systems for loading

## Market

Used Nitro 80s have sold in the range of €10,000–20,000 depending on condition, sails, and equipment. The limited production run makes them relatively rare.
